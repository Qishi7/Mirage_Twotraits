Background And Motivation

Rare-variant association studies often aggregate variants within a gene to improve power. A burden test is a common aggregation strategy, but it implicitly treats selected variants as having similar directions and comparable relevance to disease risk. That assumption can be inefficient when only a small subset of rare variants in a gene are truly associated with the trait.

MIRAGE addresses this issue with a hierarchical mixture model. It treats each gene as potentially risk-associated and, conditional on a risk gene, treats each variant as a mixture of risk and non-risk states. Functional annotation categories influence the prior probability that a variant is a risk variant. This is a more cautious use of annotation than hard filtering: annotations shift prior weights, but they do not by themselves determine whether a variant contributes evidence.

Notation

For gene \(i = 1,\ldots,G\) and variant \(j = 1,\ldots,m_i\):

  • \(X_{ij}\): allele count in cases.
  • \(X^{(0)}_{ij}\): allele count in controls.
  • \(T_{ij} = X_{ij} + X^{(0)}_{ij}\): total allele count.
  • \(N_1\): number of cases.
  • \(N_0\): number of controls.
  • \(c_{ij}\): functional annotation category for variant \(j\) in gene \(i\).
  • \(\delta\): prior probability that a gene is a risk gene.
  • \(\eta_c\): prior probability that a variant in category \(c\) is a risk variant, conditional on its gene being a risk gene.
  • \(\gamma_{ij}\): relative-risk multiplier for a risk variant.

Generative Model

The gene-level latent state is

\[ U_i \sim \operatorname{Bernoulli}(\delta), \]

where \(U_i = 1\) indicates that gene \(i\) is a risk gene.

The variant-level latent state is constrained by the gene state:

\[ Z_{ij} \mid U_i = 0 = 0, \]

and, if \(U_i = 1\),

\[ Z_{ij} \mid U_i = 1, c_{ij} = c \sim \operatorname{Bernoulli}(\eta_c). \]

Thus \(\eta_c\) is category-specific. A more deleterious annotation category may have a larger estimated \(\eta_c\), but the value is learned from the data rather than fixed solely by annotation.

Conditional Likelihood

Before conditioning, the model assumes Poisson counts with a nuisance allele frequency \(q_{ij}\). MIRAGE conditions on the total allele count \(T_{ij}\), removing \(q_{ij}\) and yielding binomial likelihoods.

For a non-risk variant,

\[ X_{ij} \mid T_{ij}, Z_{ij}=0 \sim \operatorname{Binomial} \left( T_{ij}, \frac{N_1}{N_1 + N_0} \right). \]

For a risk variant with relative-risk multiplier \(\gamma_{ij}\),

\[ X_{ij} \mid T_{ij}, Z_{ij}=1, \gamma_{ij} \sim \operatorname{Binomial} \left( T_{ij}, \frac{\gamma_{ij}N_1}{\gamma_{ij}N_1 + N_0} \right). \]

MIRAGE integrates over the prior distribution of \(\gamma_{ij}\) to compute the risk-variant marginal likelihood. The corresponding variant-level Bayes factor is

\[ B_{ij} = \frac{ P(X_{ij} \mid T_{ij}, Z_{ij}=1) }{ P(X_{ij} \mid T_{ij}, Z_{ij}=0) }. \]

EM Algorithm

The parameters \(\delta\) and \(\eta = (\eta_1,\ldots,\eta_K)\) are estimated with an empirical Bayes EM algorithm across all genes.

  1. Compute variant-level Bayes factors \(B_{ij}\).
  2. Initialize \(\delta\) and \(\eta_c\).
  3. E step: compute posterior expectations for \(U_i\) and \(U_i Z_{ij}\) under the current parameter values.
  4. M step: update \(\delta\) and each \(\eta_c\) using closed-form averages of these posterior expectations.
  5. Iterate until the parameter change is below the selected tolerance or until the maximum iteration count is reached.

In weak-signal settings, the paper notes that \(\delta\) may be difficult to identify. A fixed, scientifically motivated value of \(\delta\) may therefore be useful in sensitivity analyses.

Gene-Level Bayes Factor

Given fitted category probabilities \(\eta_c\), the gene-level Bayes factor is

\[ B_i = \prod_{j=1}^{m_i} \left[ (1 - \eta_{c_{ij}}) + \eta_{c_{ij}} B_{ij} \right]. \]

This product combines evidence across variants while allowing many variants to contribute little when their variant-level Bayes factors are close to one.

Posterior Probability

The posterior probability that gene \(i\) is a risk gene is

\[ \operatorname{PP}_i = P(U_i = 1 \mid X,T,C) = \frac{\delta B_i}{1 - \delta + \delta B_i}. \]

This posterior probability is model-based and depends on the fitted prior probability \(\delta\), the estimated annotation effects \(\eta_c\), and the variant-level evidence.

Bayesian FDR

Genes can be ranked by \(\operatorname{PP}_i\). For a selected set of genes \(S\), a Bayesian false discovery rate estimate can be written as the average posterior error probability:

\[ \operatorname{BFDR}(S) = \frac{1}{|S|} \sum_{i \in S} \left(1 - \operatorname{PP}_i\right). \]

This gives a direct posterior-probability-based multiple-testing summary. It should be interpreted under the assumptions of the MIRAGE model and the chosen prior specification.

Why MIRAGE Can Improve Over Burden Tests

MIRAGE may improve over burden tests when risk effects are sparse and heterogeneous within a gene. A burden test can dilute signal by aggregating many neutral variants with a small number of risk variants. MIRAGE instead allows each variant to contribute according to its Bayes factor and annotation-dependent prior probability.

The empirical Bayes structure can also learn that certain annotation categories tend to carry more evidence across the full gene set. This can make the model more adaptive than a fixed hard-filtering rule, although the benefit depends on annotation quality and the amount of signal in the data.

Main Limitations

  • The current model is designed for binary case-control or transmission-style count data, not quantitative traits.
  • The input data should already address population stratification through design or matching.
  • The model does not directly include sample-level covariates such as sex, age, batch, or ancestry PCs.
  • Annotation categories are treated as disjoint groups; overlapping, continuous, or high-dimensional annotations require extensions.
  • Variant independence is assumed in the gene-level product, so linkage disequilibrium should be considered during preprocessing or sensitivity analysis.
  • Weak signals can make \(\delta\) and some \(\eta_c\) values difficult to estimate precisely.
  • Results can depend on annotation definitions and relative-risk prior hyperparameters, so sensitivity analyses are important.
